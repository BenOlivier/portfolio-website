Wearable Interaction Design

Litho, London, 2022

Product DesignInteraction DesignPrototypingXRUnity

The Litho Controller is a wearable human input device, designed for spatial interactions, primarily with mobile AR.

It functions as a precise 3D pointer - like a mouse for the real world, as well as enabling gestural interactions.

Worn between the first and second fingers, the device features an array of motion-tracking sensors, a touch surface on the underside, and haptic feedback.

My work

My focus was to design and prototype new spatial interactions, interfaces and experiences for the controller which demonstrated its strengths as an input device.

These interactions were then provided to third-party developer as part of the interaction SDK which we released to developers.

Diorama filmmaking app

We built Diorama as a showpiece for the Litho controller. The app allows you to construct a scene in mixed reality, animate objects, and then record a video of your creation.

I helped concieve, design, and build Diorama, making sure we delivered an amazing experience to our users.

An earlier prototype I built of a mixed relaity shoe shopping app for Litho. The app explores the use of a gestural swipe interaction, and stencil shaders to mask objects.

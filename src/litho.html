A controller for the real world

Augmented reality has an input problem. Current solutions have us tapping and swiping at the air, wielding bulky controllers, or resorting back to our phones.

To address this, we developed the Litho Controller - a small wearable controller that sits comfortably between your index and middle fingers and connects to your phone via Bluetooth.

Litho allows for natural and precise input with its touch surface on the underside, custom haptic feedback system and array of motion-tracking sensors.

An AR product browser UI I built for Litho

SDK

We built a Unity SDK with off the shelf interactions for common 3D manipulations such as moving, scaling and rotating - allowing builders to quickly put together apps.

Our Developer Hub is a community pioneering the future of digital interaction. Have a look at some of the projects built with Litho.

We have shipped hundreds of developer kits around the world and in 2021 partnered with US startup ARKH who continue to sell the device under their own branding.
